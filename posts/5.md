In a world of constant notifications, it's easy to feel perpetually distracted. Mindfulness
doesn't require a retreat or an hour of daily meditation — small, consistent habits can
transform how you experience each day.

## Start with the Morning

Resist the urge to check your phone first thing. Spend the first ten minutes of your day
with a warm drink, a journal, or simply sitting in silence. This sets the tone for
everything that follows.

## Single-Tasking

Multitasking is a myth — what we're actually doing is task-switching, and it's exhausting.
Pick one thing, give it your full attention, then move on.

## A Gratitude Practice

Before bed, write down three things you're grateful for. They don't have to be grand —
a good cup of coffee counts. Over time this rewires your brain toward positivity.

> "Wherever you are, be all there." — Jim Elliot

Mindfulness isn't about perfection. It's about noticing when you've drifted and gently
returning — to the present, to yourself, to what matters. 🌿
